Bed Level Correction Calculator

First, complete the usual first-layer calibration and test to ensure you get good filling adhesion of the first layer, at least at the center of the bed. I recommend you also make sure your extruder is calibrated.

Second, get the test patches from this site, FirstLayerPatches.stl (or FirstLayerPatches.scad) from this site or https://www.thingiverse.com/thing:3008633. Not using 0.2mm first layer height? Not a problem... just use the Customizer (or tweak the openSCAD file) to create a .stl with the correct height. Or if your slicer can do it, scale the Z-axis only in your slicer

Third, set your slicer's first-layer height to match the .stl file and slice it using 0 perimeters and 0-degree infill.

Finally, enter the recommended bed level correction values into your machine. Give it another try and see if the patches come out more even.

Your goal in setting the bed level correction is to make the heights of the patches equal; they will very likely always measure more than the nominal first-layer thickness.

Unless you are seeing poor first-layer adhesion or the patches are more than 25% greater than the nominal first-layer height, changing the live-adjust Z may not be necessary. Be aware that the thickness of the layers increases both when the height is too high and when the height is too low!
